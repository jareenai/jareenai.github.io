About me...

As a producer for CNN, Jareen Imam creates compelling content for the network's digital and television platforms. She's a leader in the newsroom as well as a team player. Her expertise includes: managing online communities, engaging digital audiences, verifying and soliciting user-generated content, developing innovative projects for social media, producing content that is specific for digital and social platforms, creating and launching social media campaigns, discovering online trends and investigative reporting.

Imam first joined the company in January 2011 as an intern for CNN iReport team. Six months later, she became a video journalist and editorial assistant, working for CNN, CNN International and HLN's television networks. In 2012, Imam moved to CNN Digital as an associate producer.

Currently, Imam works with the CNN Social Discovery team as a producer where her responsibilities include managing social media accounts for CNN (such as CNN & CNN iReport's Facebook, Twitter, Tumblr and Instagram accounts), working with CNN's Snapchat team, finding and producing trending stories for the network as well as creating community-driven, user generated assignments for online communities.

Imam is a graduate of Emory University, with a triple major in English, creative writing and journalism. She also has a strong background in science and economics. While attending the university, Imam was the managing editor of Emory's Political Review, editor of Emory's satirical magazine, and a regular contributor to the school's popular newspaper.

Outside of work, Imam is an active member in the Atlanta community and volunteers her time at Literacy Action, a nonprofit, teaching reading and writing skills to adults. She is also a mentor for aspiring teen writers and photographers at VOX Teen Communications.
