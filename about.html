About me...

As the Head of Social at CBS News, Jareen Imam leads a team of producers who create social and digital videos, write articles and program content across various social platforms. Imam, who is based in New York City, is charged with not only leading a team of exceptional journalists, but is also responsible for creating and implementing social strategies that focus on growing, engaging and reaching new audiences.

Prior to joining CBS News, Imam was the Social Discovery Producer for CNN in Atlanta. Her responsibilities included discovering, reporting and investigating trending stories. In this role, Imam has worked as an editor for CNN's Snapchat Discover Channel, covered breaking and live events for CNN's Facebook, Twitter and Instagram accounts and worked on new social platforms to curate viral content for the network.

Before joining CNN Social, Imam worked with the award-winning CNN iReport team where she focused on audience engagement and community management. Her expertise includes launching user-generated projects, creating social media campaigns and creating social content for emerging platforms.

Imam also previously managed and edited CNN's space and science section, assigning, writing and field reporting coverage. Imam started her journalism career as an intern and then freelancer with the documentary crime series "48 Hours," conducting field investigations and research for the television show.

Imam received a bachelor's degree in English, creative writing and journalism from Emory University, and was recognized with honors for her achievements in the arts. During her enrollment, Imam was the managing editor of Emory's Political Review, editor of the college's satirical magazine and a staff writer for university's newspaper.
